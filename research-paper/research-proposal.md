Are Prestigious Universities Still Worth the
Cost in the 21st Century?
1.Research question - This research aims to investigate
whether prestigious university education continues to provide a positive
return on
investment for students in the 21st century.
2. Why Does This Matter?
Many students begin thinking about their future careers during high
school. For many of them, attending university is considered the most
important step towards professional success. However, higher education
often requires significant financial investment, while tuition fees and
living costs continue to increase.
As a result, students and their families are increasingly asking whether a
university degree still provides sufficient value for its cost. While higher
education remains beneficial in many cases, alternative pathways such as
vocational training, apprenticeships, and entrepreneurship have become
more accessible and attractive.
This research aims to examine whether prestigious universities continue
to offer a strong return on investment for students in the 21st century. It
also seeks to explore situations in which alternative educational pathways
may provide comparable or even greater economic benefits.
3. Hypothesis
Prestigious universities provide positive returns for some students, but
may not always be the most financially efficient pathway compared to
alternative educational options.
4. Countries
UK, USA, Germany, Netherlands, Russia
5. Metrics
Student Perspective
• Tuition Cost
• Living Cost
• Graduate Salary
• Estimated Payback Period
Government Perspective
• Tax Revenue
• Human Capital Development
• Economic Productivity
6. Expected outcome
The research will identify circumstances in which higher education
provides strong economic returns and situations where alternative
educational pathways may be more beneficial.
